Engagement Report

Pendo · Product Analytics

Terms used in the weekly and baseline engagement analysis of the VMS (Bloomerang Volunteer) application, sourced from Pendo activity and PES data.

Active Visitors Metric

The count of unique visitors who performed at least one tracked interaction within the VMS application during a given time period. Used as the primary measure of weekly reach.

In this report: compared weekly against a 6-month baseline average of ~2,308/week.

6-Month Baseline Metric

The reference period of Sep 26, 2025 – Mar 25, 2026 (26 weeks) used to establish normal pre-launch behavior. All post-launch metrics are compared against weekly averages derived from this window to smooth out single-week anomalies.

PES Metric

Product Engagement Score. A composite Pendo score (0–100) that combines Adoption, Stickiness, and Growth into a single measure of how deeply users are engaging with the product over a time period.

PES = f(Adoption, Stickiness, Growth)

6-month baseline average: ~20.5. Launch week: 19.0 (−7%).

Adoption Score Metric

The percentage of the eligible user base that used core tracked features or pages within the period. Measures breadth of feature usage across accounts or visitors.

6-month baseline average: ~26.6. Launch week: 21.7 (−18%).

Stickiness Score Metric

The ratio of active users in a shorter window (e.g., daily or weekly) to active users in a longer window (e.g., monthly). A higher stickiness score indicates users are returning more frequently relative to the total active user base.

6-month baseline average: ~13.3. Launch week: 19.7 (+48%) — the standout positive signal in the launch-week data.

Growth Score Metric

A measure of net new user acquisition — how many new visitors or accounts are being added relative to churned ones. Reflects whether the product is expanding its active user base.

6-month baseline average: ~23.2. Launch week: 15.7 (−32%) — the sharpest drop in launch week, worth monitoring over subsequent weeks.

Page Events (numEvents) Metric

Total number of page load or page view events recorded on a given VMS page during the period. Includes repeat visits by the same user. Used to assess overall traffic volume to specific areas of the product.

Unique Visitors Metric

The deduplicated count of individual users (identified by Pendo visitor ID) who accessed a page or feature within the period, regardless of how many times they visited.

Unique Accounts Metric

The deduplicated count of customer organizations (accounts) that had at least one visitor active on a given page or feature during the period. Useful for understanding breadth of adoption across the customer base rather than individual users.

Opp Dashboard VMS Page

The Opportunity-level dashboard page within VMS (page: VMS - Opportunity - Dashboard). A new page introduced as part of the March 26 launch that became one of the top-trafficked pages in the first week, indicating the protocol redesign is routing users through a new primary entry point.

Org Level VMS Context

The organization layer of VMS, where volunteer managers oversee a nonprofit organization's full volunteer program, including all opportunities, volunteer lists, schedules, and settings. Distinct from the Opportunity level, which scopes to a single event or initiative.

Opp Level VMS Context

The opportunity layer of VMS, scoped to a specific volunteer opportunity (event, program, or initiative). Includes pages for Schedule, Volunteers, Signup Workflow, Roles, General settings, and the Opportunity Dashboard.

Recruitment Page VMS Page

The public-facing opportunity recruitment pages within VMS, covering the public sign-up page, form questions, and the signup workflow. Was the #2 most visited page pre-launch; declined in relative ranking post-launch as the new Opp Dashboard absorbed entry-point traffic.

Top Nav Icons Navigation

Icon-based navigation elements that appear in the top navigation bar at the opportunity level, linking to Schedule, Volunteer List, Settings, Roles & Qualifications, Recruitment, and Email Communications sections. Pre-launch, these were the most-clicked features by unique visitor count. Post-launch, their usage dropped significantly, suggesting the new navigation architecture routes users differently.

Protocol Guide Coverage Report

Pendo · In-App Guides

Terms used in the guide performance analysis, drawn from Pendo guide metrics and the Notion In-App Messages Database where each guide's spec is documented.

Guide Pendo

An in-app message or walkthrough built and delivered via Pendo. Guides are triggered based on audience targeting rules (page, segment, date/time) and appear to users inside the Bloomerang Volunteer application. Per internal guidelines, guides are treated as extensions of the product and must inform rather than disrupt.

IAM Nomenclature

In-App Message — the internal tracking ID prefix (e.g., IAM-459, IAM-618) assigned automatically in the Notion In-App Messages Database when a new guide request is created. Used to reference a specific guide across Pendo, Notion, and team discussions.

T0 — Entry Modal Guide Tier

The first tier of the protocol guide system. T0 guides are multi-step progressive onboarding modals shown to users on or shortly after launch day. Their purpose is to orient users to the new navigation and prompt them to begin a guided tour. Two T0 guides exist: one for Full Org Managers and one for Role Managers.

Launch week data: 419 and 387 unique viewers respectively; dismissal rates of 70% and 62%. No completions recorded — consistent with notification-style modals that lack a discrete completion step.

T1 — Platform Orientation Guide Tier

The second tier. T1 guides provide a high-level orientation to the platform, surfaced from the top navigation. Two variants exist: one for Organization Managers and one for Role Managers. Activated programmatically from a top nav item.

Launch week data: 101 and 2 unique viewers; ~10% completion rate and <8% dismissal rate — healthy engagement for users who reach them.

T2 — Area Tour Guide Tier

The third tier. T2 guides are multi-step tours of a specific area of the product — currently the Org Tour and the Opp Level Overview. These are longer walkthroughs (11+ views per user) designed to familiarize users with a functional section of VMS.

Launch week data: 402 and 154 unique viewers; 7.5–9.9% completion; 8.5–11.6% dismissal; 8–11 views per user — the best-performing guides in the protocol.

T3 — Feature Walkthrough Guide Tier

The fourth and most granular tier. T3 guides are deep, feature-specific walkthroughs covering individual VMS functions such as Volunteer List, Schedule, Roles, Tags, Signup Workflow, Qualifications, Template Reports, Email Automations, Form & Profile Fields, Importing, and Attendance. Available at both Org and Opp levels.

Launch week data: most T3 guides had low reach (1–27 viewers) as they are contextually triggered. Completion rates of 6–25% where data is sufficient. Dismissal rates generally low (<15%).

Total Views Metric

The total number of times a guide was displayed to any user during the period, including repeat views by the same user. A single user seeing a multi-step guide multiple times contributes multiple views.

Unique Viewers Metric

The deduplicated count of individual users who were shown a guide at least once during the period, regardless of how many times they viewed it.

Views Per User (VPU) Metric

The average number of times each unique viewer saw a guide during the period. A high VPU indicates either a multi-step guide that users are progressing through (positive) or a guide that users repeatedly encounter without completing (potentially negative).

VPU = Total Views ÷ Unique Viewers

In this report: VPU of 8+ is treated as "deep engagement" (blue); 3–7 as moderate (amber); below 3 as shallow (gray).

Completion Rate Metric

The percentage of total guide views that resulted in a completion event — typically clicking the final CTA or reaching the last step of a walkthrough. A 0% completion rate does not necessarily indicate failure; notification-style or banner guides have no completion step by design.

Completion Rate = Completions ÷ Total Views × 100

Dismissal Rate Signal

The percentage of total guide views in which the user explicitly closed or dismissed the guide before completing it. A high dismissal rate (especially >50%) on entry-point guides is a signal that the guide's timing, framing, or content is creating friction rather than value at first contact.

Dismissal Rate = Dismissals ÷ Total Views × 100

In this report: rates ≥50% are flagged red; 25–49% amber; <25% green.

NPS Survey (Guide) Guide Type

A Pendo guide configured as a Net Promoter Score poll. Users are asked how likely they are to recommend Bloomerang Volunteer to a friend or colleague on a 0–10 scale. Responses are classified as Detractors (0–6), Neutrals (7–8), or Promoters (9–10).

NPS = % Promoters − % Detractors

Launch week: NPS of −50 from 6 responses (4 detractors, 1 neutral, 1 promoter). Statistically limited — directional only until volume grows.

Reach Tier Classification

A classification applied in this report to group guides by their unique viewer count during launch week, enabling more meaningful comparison within peer groups.

High50+ unique viewers
Mid20–49 unique viewers
Low1–19 unique viewers
Pop-up Modal Guide Format

A guide format that appears as a smaller overlay on the page, typically anchored near a specific UI element or appearing in a corner. Less intrusive than a center screen modal. Used for the Day Of and Week Out URL navigation notification guides (IAM-459, IAM-586).

Center Screen Modal Guide Format

A guide format that appears as a full overlay centered on the screen, blocking interaction with the underlying page until dismissed. Designed to be impossible to miss. Used for IAM-618 (the "Take the Tour" CTA on launch day). Per the Notion spec: "We don't want them to miss or simply dismiss this message."

Snooze / Remind Me Guide Feature

A guide interaction option that dismisses the guide and suppresses it for 1 day, after which it reappears. Both the Day Of navigation guides (IAM-459 and IAM-618) included a snooze option. Contributes to repeat views (higher VPU) without constituting a dismissal.

Go Live Date Property

The date a guide became active in Pendo and started being shown to users. For the launch cohort: IAM-618 went live Mar 26, 2026; IAM-459 went live Mar 30, 2026. Tracked as a property in the Notion In-App Messages Database.

End Date Property

The scheduled date on which a guide will be deactivated and stop appearing to users. Both Day Of navigation guides are scheduled to end Apr 24, 2026 — approximately four weeks post-launch.

Org Manager User Role

A VMS user with full administrative access to an organization's volunteer program. Can manage all opportunities, volunteers, schedules, settings, and reports within an organization. Targeted by the Full Org Manager T0 and T1 guides.

Role Manager User Role

A VMS user with permissions scoped to managing specific roles or opportunities within an organization, rather than the full org. Targeted by the Role Manager T0 and T1 guides. Also referred to as "Opportunity & Limited Org Manager" in some guide specs.

Guide Category: Operational Classification

A guide category defined in the Notion In-App Messages Database indicating that the guide's purpose is operational — communicating product changes, navigation updates, or workflow instructions — as opposed to promotional or marketing-driven content.

Both IAM-459 and IAM-618 are categorized as Operational, requested jointly by Marketing and Product.

URL Navigation Update Context

The underlying product change that triggered the launch-week protocol guides. The Bloomerang Volunteer application's navigation structure — including URL routing — was redesigned as part of the March 26, 2026 release. The goal, per guide copy: "Fewer clicks, clearer labels, and a layout designed around your day-to-day tasks."
